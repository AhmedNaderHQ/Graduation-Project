Project Objectives

The number of accidents has significantly increased due to driver distraction, fatigue, or boredom, making driving more challenging. With the continuous efforts of the government and the Ministry of Transportation to develop roads and bridges to reduce accidents, improve the driving experience, save time, and shorten distances, we propose a project aimed at reducing risks, saving lives, and making driving safer and more enjoyable for drivers.

The System Includes:

Do Not Pass Warning System

Forward Collision Warning System

Blind Spot Warning System

Emergency Electronic Brake Light System

Intersection Movement Assist

Driver Monitoring System

Driver Face Recognition

Project Description

Do Not Pass Warning (DNPW)

This system senses the driver’s condition and alerts them when feeling drowsy or experiencing unstable health conditions, ensuring their safety and that of the passengers.

Forward Collision Warning (FCW)

This system aims to warn the driver of any potential collision with vehicles or obstacles ahead, allowing them to avoid potential accidents by reducing response time.

Blind Spot Warning (BSW)

This system alerts the driver to vehicles or obstacles in the blind spot, enhancing safety during lane changes.

Emergency Electronic Brake Light System (EEBL)

This feature is activated during sudden braking to alert other vehicles on the road, reducing the risk of rear-end collisions.

Intersection Movement Assist (IMA)

This system monitors intersections and alerts the driver to potential hazards, such as oncoming vehicles from the sides.

Driver Monitoring System (DMS)

This system senses the driver’s condition and alerts them when feeling drowsy or experiencing unstable health conditions.

Driver Face Recognition (DFR)

This system uses an internal camera to ensure that the person entering the vehicle is the authorized driver.
